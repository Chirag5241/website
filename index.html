Can we reliably curb escalation risk in competing models without sacrificing strategic effectiveness?

I aim to build interpretable, controllable best-response models to study cooperative, deceptive, exploitative, and escalatory behavior. I aim to isolate interpretable escalation signals and intervene to stop spirals. I aim to apply this to negotiation and wargaming at a time when great-power dynamics increasingly recast dangerous crises as “windows of opportunity"

About me

I am a Software Engineer at Qualcomm, developing modem data software and leading IP Accelerator driver features for Linux. I hold a B.S. in Computer Engineering from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign.

My research focuses on interpretable AI and strategic reasoning. I've published work on causal probing in language models, multimodal fusion for autonomous navigation, and self-supervised texture segmentation—spanning venues from IJCNLP-AACL to IEEE Robotics and Automation Letters.

From kernel development to machine learning pipelines, I build systems that bridge low-level performance engineering with high-level intelligent decision-making.
